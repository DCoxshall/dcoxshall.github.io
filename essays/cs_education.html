Why are there so many people on my course? And why don't any of them have basic computer skills?

Computer science students are bad at computer science...

When I arrived at university, I had expected to immediately be surrounded by young people who were at least as good as I am, when it comes to technology. Not that I'm some kind of benchmark - I just underestimated how interested in computers the average computer science student would be.

And you know what? I can understand that. I can understand not having an active interest in your degree. I get that writing code for 12 hours a day is perhaps not to everyone's taste. I get that. But I had kind of expected people to have basic IT skils. I had expected people to be able to do things like navigate a Linux shell, to be able to change environment variables on Windows, to be able to install software without needing a lecturers help.

But they can't.

As I'm writing this, I've just finished a module involving development of a full-stack application, with Spring Boot on the backend and Angular on the frontend. I've never used either of those frameworks.

And to be fair, the module was designed to account for that. Spring and Angular were wrapped up in a nice little piece of malware called JHipster, which is a "tool" designed to help web developers prototype applications as quickly as possible. If you're an experienced dev in need a quick MVP, I imagine JHipster can be quite useful.

...but it's not their fault.
